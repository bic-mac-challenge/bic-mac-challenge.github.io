About the Challenge

Task 1: CT-Space Evaluation

Generate pseudo-CT attenuation maps evaluated in CT-space. Performance is quantified by the mean absolute relative error between the synthesized pseudo-mu-map and the reference CT-derived mu-map.

Task 2: PET-Space Evaluation

Generate attenuation maps evaluated in PET-space with closed-loop reconstruction. Performance is quantified by comparing the pseudo-AC PET with the reference CT-AC PET using STIR reconstruction software.

Challenge Abstract

The advent of Long Axial Field-of-View (LAFOV) PET scanners has shifted the dosimetry paradigm in PET/CT imaging. The high sensitivity of these systems allows for substantial reductions in radiotracer activity, rendering the volumetric CT component the dominant source of ionizing radiation. To facilitate ultra-low dose PET protocols, which are preferred for pediatric and obstetric cohorts, it can be desirable to eliminate the volumetric CT entirely. However, in addition to providing anatomical context, the CT is needed for the attenuation correction (AC) and reconstruction of the PET image, as the attenuation map (mu-map) is often derived directly from the CT.

Addressing this, the Big Cross-Modal Attenuation Correction (BIC-MAC) challenge tasks participants with synthesizing a 3D pseudo-CT (mu-map) in the PET coordinate space using other available modalities. We present a multimodal dataset comprising whole-body PET, CT, Topogram (CT-scout), and MRI for 100 healthy volunteers. The cohort is age- and sex-stratified, and data were acquired on modern Siemens Quadra and Siemens VIDA scanners. Participants will be provided with a training set of 80 Non-Attenuation Corrected (NAC) 18F-FDG PET images, scatter-maps, and sinograms, scan-planning Topograms, and same-day MRI DIXON images, alongside the reference CT-based mu-maps and the reference CT-AC PET images.

The challenge comprises two tasks which both concern the generation of a mu-map, but which are evaluated in either CT-space or PET-space. For the CT-space, performance is quantified by the mean absolute relative error between the pseudo-mu-map and CT-mu-map, and for the PET-space, between the pseudo-AC PET and CT-AC PET. In the latter case, a docker image is provided with STIR PET reconstruction software to enable closed loop optimization on the training set.

A defining technical characteristic of this challenge is the integration of modalities of differing dimensions acquired in differing geometries. While the 3D NAC-PET and 2D Topograms are spatially aligned with the target mu-map, both lack anatomical fidelity. In contrast, whole-body MRI offers high bone and soft tissue contrast but is acquired in a different scanner geometry with different body deformations. Consequently, to approximate the mu-map accurately, participants must develop algorithms capable of fusing the unaligned information from 3D volumetric MRI with that of the 3D NAC-PET and 2D Topograms.
